Case Study

The August 2025 Afghanistan Earthquake

In August 2025, a magnitude 6.0 earthquake struck northeastern Afghanistan (Kuz Kunar District). Ground communications were severed, leaving responders blind.

The Space Solution: UN-SPIDER activated the Jilin-1 satellite constellation. By leveraging AI to analyze high-resolution imagery, the system generated a precise damage assessment report in less than 4 hours.

  • Rapid scanning of thousands of images.
  • Generation of heatmaps guiding rescue teams.

How It Works

The three pillars of space technology for disaster management.

1. The Eyes

Earth Observation

Satellites monitor the "greenness" of plants to predict droughts and track floods in real-time.

2. The Map

Satellite Navigation

GPS and Galileo provide the precise coordinates needed to guide rescue teams to damaged areas.

3. The Voice

SatCom

When cell towers fall, satellites provide the only reliable link for coordinating international aid.
